Education

In my third semester at Tech, I'm delving into upper level computer science courses and truly making Atlanta a second home.

My journey

After graduating from Fairfield Warde High School in June of 2017, I left Connecticut to attend the Georgia Institute of Technology as part of the Honors Program.

I was fortunate enough to be awarded the Stamps Presidential Scholarship — a full-ride merit based scholarship offered annually to the top one percent of incoming freshman.

Preceding the start of the fall semester, our cohort went caving and trekked through Scotland on the West Highland Way.

Studies

Georgia Tech’s unique threads program, offered by the College of Computing, has allowed me to specialize in my interests.

  • Coursework

    So far, my relevant courses include Machine Learning, Data Structures & Algorithms, Object-oriented Programming, Discrete Math, Multivariable Calculus, Linear Algebra, and Global Issues & Leadership.

  • Threads

    My two concentrations are People and Intelligence, which together have enabled me to focus on AI and human–computer interaction.

  • Path

    With my current academic track and minor in Philosophy, I plan to graduate in May of 2021.

  • Online

    I'm a big fan of self-motivated education, and have learned a lot through online courses in my discipline. This past summer I completed Andrew Ng's Deep Learning Specialization, and uploaded personal notes and tutorials for others to use. View my certificate here.
